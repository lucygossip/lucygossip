No Matter Where You Are

‘No Matter Where You Are’ is a composition for choir and piano. It is inspired by the book ‘Dear John’ by Nicholas Sparks. This piece was created early on in the coronavirus lockdown. It was written partly as a reminder that we are all still connected; but more importantly, it enables the choir to put its voices together even during a time when we’re forced to sing in isolation.

The lyrics are based around quotes from the novel:

‘No matter where you are in the world, the moon is never bigger than your thumb.’

‘It’s a full moon here tonight, which makes me think of you. Because I know…no matter what I am doing, or where I am, this moon will always be the same size as yours, half a world away.’

Mrs Beeton

London 1860s. Curbing her husband’s spending was never going to be easy, especially when newlywed Isabella Beeton is battling her elderly cook, penning an epic romance and running a publishing company. If she can pay off the debts, and bring her husband to heel, can she start living her happily ever after?

'Mrs Beeton' is a musical written by Zella Compton and Lucy Gossip. These excerpts from the show were performed by musical theatre students from the University of Chichester Conservatoire. The performance took place at Crazy Coqs, as part of a showcase by the organisation Book, Music and Lyrics.

The Art of Perfection

'The Art of Perfection' was created as a Masters project at Leeds College of Music. It is an original story about a fictional character, Suzanna, who has an obsession with perfection that causes her to descend into madness. The piece has three parts, each approximately ten minutes long.

Part one depicts Suzanna's life as told through a musical response to a variety of topics in a newspaper.

Part two is an analysis of Suzanna's attitude towards perfection, as compared to the attitude of those around her.

Part three depicts Suzanna's unobtainable perfect goal, which results in her obsession descending into madness.
